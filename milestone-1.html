Introduction

01

EDI (Equity, Diversity, and Inclusion) has become a major focus in higher education to ensure fair and meaningful learning for all students

02

In mathematics education, EDI is especially important because the subject is seen as neutral, yet hidden barriers can still exclude certain groups.

03

The study investigates how EDI principles are applied, perceived and experienced by mathematics majors across various universities.

04

Research shows that gender bias, lack of representation, socio-cultural stereotypes by mathematics mahors across various universities

05

These barriers negatively impact students' confidencem engagement, and academic success.

06

The study aims to evaluate how inclusive current mathematics learning environments truly are.

07

Findings will help identify improvements needed to increase diversity and support in mathematics programs.

08

Mathematics majors' experiences are crucial since they will becomw future educators, researchers, and professionals shaping the next generation of learners.
