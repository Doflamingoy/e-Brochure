Summary Report

Accessibility
  • Most students report adequate learning facilities, including math laboratories (52%) and supportive classrooms and libraries (85%).
  • A large majority (83%) say sufficient printed and digital math references are available.
  • Learning tools and teaching methods are generally accessible, with 68% noting materials availability and 65% acknowledging diverse instructional approaches.
Diversity
  • Students feel diversity enhances collaboration and problem-solving, with 64% agreeing that peer diversity benefits learning.
  • Professors frequently use culturally relevant examples (64%), supporting inclusive engagement.
  • While 78% see equal participation opportunities, neutral responses about teaching styles suggest room for improved differentiated instruction.
Inclusivity
  • Most students view the institution as inclusive, with 71% seeing a welcoming environment and 84% acknowledging fair opportunities.
  • Policies and classroom practices reflect fairness, supported by 79% and 85% agreement, respectively.
  • Diverse student organizations (87%) further promote belonging and inclusivity.

Conclusion

  • Students hold positive perceptions of accessibility, diversity, and inclusivity in mathematics education.
  • Facilities, teaching practices, and institutional policies support fairness and engagement.
  • Neutral responses highlight areas for improvement, especially in consistent differentiated instruction.

Overall Interpretation

  • Students generally agree that learning resources are accessible and teachers support varied learning needs.
  • Diversity is recognized as valuable for collaboration, fairness, and problem-solving.
