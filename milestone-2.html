Milestone 2

Tour to know: Freshmen Impression on the Learning Environment and Resources

Background and Rationale

Inclusive mathematics education values human diversity and ensures that all students can progress academically and develop holistically

EDI (Equity, Diversity, and Inclusion) supports respectful, safe, and equitable learning environments where all students feel valued and included.

Inclusive math classrooms use varied pedagogical approaches and teaching resources that allow all learners—regardless of background or ability—to participate meaningfully.

Some university students still report inadequate learning resources and facilities, highlighting the need for continued support to strengthen inclusive practices.

First-year university students will conduct a campus tour to observe learning facilities, gather data, and assess how these resources influence engagement and sense of belonging.

Findings from the visit aim to provide recommendations for improving inclusive mathematics education, supporting student engagement, and enhancing campus learning environments.

Description of the Activity / Project

The “Tour to Know: Mathematics Freshmen's Impressions of the Learning Environment and Resources” is an observational campus tour for students. Guided by an admission officer, participants will visit university facilities and observe real classroom lessons. The tour aims to help students evaluate the learning environment, available resources, and teaching approaches to determine whether the institution meets their academic needs.

  • Target Participants: Freshmen Students
  • Key Activities: Campus Tour and Observational Visit
    1. Orientation and Introduction
    2. Interactive discussion and reflection
    3. Feedback and Evaluation

Objectives / Expected Outcomes

1. To increase first-year students’ awareness of the school’s learning environment and available academic resources by 80%, as measured through post-tour feedback and reflection forms collected at the end of the activity.

2. To assess and document at least three areas for improvement in the inclusivity and accessibility of learning spaces, based on the analysis of student feedback and observation reports gathered during the tour.

Theoretical and Scholarly Support

  1. According to Vithal (2023), A systematic review of equity-related studies in mathematics education from 2017 to 2022 was conducted, revealing five main topics: equity understanding, fair teaching practices, curriculum availability, and system practices.
  2. According to Abtahi & Planas (2024), A framework has been established that connects EDI with mathematics instruction and teacher preparation to fight against exclusion.
  3. According to Wang et al. (2023), Social Cognitive Career Theory was applied to analyze gender disparities in STEM interest, and it was found that self-efficacy, social support, and exposure have distinct effects on the engagement of males and females.
